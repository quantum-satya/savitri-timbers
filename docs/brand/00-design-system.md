# Purpose

The Savitri Timbers Design System establishes a unified framework for designing, developing and maintaining all digital products associated with the company.

Its purpose is to ensure that every website page, user interface component and future digital product presents a consistent visual identity, user experience and technical quality.

The Design System serves as the single source of truth for design and frontend implementation.

---

# Vision

Core Principle

Business follows trust. Trust begins with every experience.

Create a timeless, premium and maintainable digital experience that reflects the quality, reliability and professionalism of Savitri Timbers.

The Design System should enable future growth without compromising consistency.

---

# Objectives

The Design System has the following objectives.

- Ensure visual consistency.
- Improve user experience.
- Strengthen brand identity.
- Reduce design duplication.
- Simplify frontend development.
- Improve maintainability.
- Support AI-assisted development.
- Accelerate future product development.

---

# Scope

This Design System applies to:

- Corporate Website
- Product Pages
- Sports Flooring Pages
- Landing Pages
- Future Dealer Portal
- Future Customer Portal
- Future Mobile Applications
- Internal Digital Tools

unless explicitly stated otherwise.

---

# Guiding Principles

Every design decision should support one or more of the following principles.

## Trust

The website should inspire confidence.

---

## Simplicity

Simple solutions are preferred over complex ones.

---

## Consistency

Established patterns should always be reused.

---

## Performance

Fast experiences create better user experiences.

---

## Accessibility

Every visitor should be able to use the website comfortably.

---

## Maintainability

The system should remain easy to understand and extend.

---

## Scalability

The Design System should support the company's future growth.

# Governance

Business decisions remain the responsibility of Savitri Timbers.

Technical implementation should follow the Design System.

If conflicts arise:

Business requirements take precedence.

---

# Change Management

Every proposed change should answer:

Why is the change needed?

What problem does it solve?

Will it improve consistency?

Will it improve usability?

Will it simplify maintenance?

If the answer is "No", the change should not be introduced.

---

# AI Assisted Development

AI is considered a development assistant.

AI may:

- Suggest improvements.
- Generate code.
- Refactor components.
- Improve accessibility.
- Improve performance.

AI must not:

- Redesign the website without approval.
- Introduce new design languages.
- Replace existing components unnecessarily.
- Ignore documented standards.

AI should always consult:

- PROJECT.md
- AI-INSTRUCTIONS.md
- BRAND-GUIDE.md

before proposing major changes.

---

# Component First Philosophy

Every page should be assembled from reusable components.

Examples include:

- Navigation
- Hero Section
- Featured Business Unit
- Product Card
- CTA Banner
- Information Card
- Contact Block
- Footer

New components should only be introduced when an existing component cannot satisfy the requirement.

# Documentation Standards

Every chapter should include:

- Principles
- Standards
- Examples

- Related Documents

This ensures consistency throughout the Design System.

---

# Review Process

Before approving a design change, verify:

- Business alignment
- Brand consistency
- Accessibility
- Mobile responsiveness
- Performance
- Maintainability

Every major design change should be reviewed before implementation.

---

# Success Criteria

The Design System is considered successful if it:

- Produces a consistent visual identity.
- Reduces duplicated design work.
- Simplifies frontend development.
- Improves maintainability.
- Enables AI to generate consistent interfaces.
- Supports future digital products.
